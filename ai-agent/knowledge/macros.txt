Macronutrients (Macros) - Nutrition Basics

Macronutrients are nutrients the body needs in large amounts: protein, carbohydrates, and fats.

Protein (4 calories per gram):
- Essential for muscle repair, immune function, and satiety
- Recommended: 0.8g per kg body weight for sedentary adults; 1.2-2.0g for active individuals or those building muscle
- Sources: lean meat, fish, eggs, legumes, tofu, Greek yogurt

Carbohydrates (4 calories per gram):
- Primary energy source for the brain and muscles
- Complex carbs (whole grains, vegetables) provide sustained energy; simple carbs (sugar) cause blood sugar spikes
- Recommended: 45-65% of daily calories; focus on fiber-rich sources (25-30g fiber daily)

Fats (9 calories per gram):
- Essential for hormone production, vitamin absorption, and cell health
- Prioritize unsaturated fats (olive oil, nuts, avocado, fatty fish) over saturated and trans fats
- Recommended: 20-35% of daily calories

For weight loss: moderate deficit (300-500 cal), adequate protein to preserve muscle, reduce refined carbs.
For weight gain: surplus with strength training, prioritize protein and complex carbs.
